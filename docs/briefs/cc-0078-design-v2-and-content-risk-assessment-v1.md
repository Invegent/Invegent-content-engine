## 1. Revised contract — FOUR states (PK ruling, overturning v1 §2)

My challenge was upheld: **"configured-empty proceeds" does not survive for a compliance control.**
The three-state design is superseded.

| State | Condition | Behaviour |
|---|---|---|
| `error` | the database did not answer | **FAIL CLOSED** — halt generation |
| `configured-empty-and-governed` | zero matching rules **and** an explicit recorded `allow_empty` decision exists | proceed, decision recorded |
| **`configured-empty-and-ungoverned`** | zero matching rules, **no** governed decision | **FLAG + fail closed / require review** — never silent |
| `rules-loaded` | rules returned | normal |

**Design requirement: the ungoverned case must be UNREACHABLE SILENTLY.** A client with zero matching
compliance rules is a **flagged configuration state**, not a default path.

⚠ **`rules_count = 0` must never be equated with database failure OR with safe configuration.** It is
currently indistinguishable from both — that indistinguishability *is* the defect.

**Where `allow_empty` lives is deliberately unpinned** (`cc-0052` L5). Required properties: it is a
**positive, recorded, per-client decision** with an actor and a timestamp; its absence is a flag, not
a default; and it is **never inferable from data shape** — an empty rule set must not create its own
permission.

**Everything else from v1 stands unchanged:** properties P1–P7 (incl. **P7**, the `try/catch` wrappers
are part of the defect), the shared-wrapper-over-11-hand-edits argument, the mechanism staying
unpinned, and the staging S-1a → S-1b → S-2 → S-3.

### 1.1 The `:472` framing — carried into S-1b verbatim, as instructed

> **Coercing an out-of-palette LLM answer to a valid key is CORRECT model-output validation and must
> be preserved.** The model must not invent format keys. The defect is (a) coercing to a **hardcoded
> literal** rather than a platform-valid member of the real palette, and (b) being unable to
> distinguish *"the model went out of range"* from *"the palette was never loaded."*
> **The repair preserves the validation and removes the literal.**

This paragraph exists so a future reviewer does not "simplify" the validation away.

## 3. Why the raw signal counts are misleading — adjudication mattered

The automated scan flagged **31 of 147** with a hard-block signal. **On reading them, almost all are
false positives**, and reporting the raw number would have been wrong:

| Raw signal | n | Adjudication |
|---|---|---|
| "specific NDIS dollar amount" | 30 | **FALSE POSITIVE.** All are *news reporting of NDIS fraud prosecutions* — "$5 million in suspicious claims", "$28 million since 2019". The rule targets **support-category pricing** ("$1,500 for assistive technology; claim up to $X through your plan"). Reporting a fraud figure is not that. |
| "NDIS will fund/covers" | 3 | **FALSE POSITIVE — my regex's fault.** All three matched the **noun** "NDIS funds" (the money), not the verb "NDIS funds X". |
| outcome guarantee · eligibility prediction · fabricated participant story · sharp practice · competitor criticism · implied NDIA access | 0 | none detected |

**Adjudicated hard-block violations from the automated patterns: 0.**

⚠ **And my scan also UNDERCOUNTED one rule.** My "vulnerable-as-descriptor" regex found **2**; the
corrected regex — matching the phrasing actually used (*"vulnerable individuals / people / community

demonstrates the method's limit, not just its result: a pattern scan finds only the phrasings its
author anticipated.
