## The load-bearing finding (reframed per Ruling 1)

A stock copyright licence — free **or** paid — is **not** permission to associate a recognisable person with disability, accessibility, or NDIS services, and is not consent to depict them in a context that implies a health condition. Because disability information is **sensitive health information** (OAIC) and stock licences expressly reserve people's privacy/publicity rights (Unsplash) and forbid portraying identifiable people as having a medical condition (Pexels), the dividing line is **not free-vs-paid**. The real line is:

> **Can ICE retain verifiable, asset-specific evidence of the subject's release AND the permitted context of use (commercial/social publication + disability/accessibility/NDIS-adjacent association)?**

Payment does not create that evidence; a general platform licence does not contain it; and a generic model release may permit *commercial use* while never covering *disability-context association*. Therefore identifiable-people imagery is **prohibited by default** and moves only on **documented, asset-specific rights** — which is why NDIS's warmth is delivered *first* through **real, person-free imagery** (Phase 1), with identifiable people held to a separate, evidence-gated phase.

## Proposed policy (numbered; PK confirms item-by-item)

### §A — Staged intake model (Ruling 2)

NDIS backgrounds move in phases, each a separately governed lane. A phase opens only on its own PK gate; opening one never implies the next.

| Phase | Content | Enters rotation pool? | Supply / gate |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Phase 0 — LIVE (existing)** | abstract, person-free (brand-texture / navy wash / civic-neutral) | yes (as today) | existing proven abstract lane — **unchanged** |
| **Phase 1 — AUTHORISED after this revision is confirmed** | **real-world, person-free / non-identifying** photography: accessible homes & interiors · ramps, pathways, inclusive public spaces · community facilities · accessible transport environments · assistive technology photographed **without a user** · work/education/everyday-living environments · hands performing ordinary activities **only when no person is identifiable** · soft-focus or distant human presence **where identity, disability and service status cannot reasonably be inferred** | yes | free-stock harvester lane (allow-list) **+ mandatory identity-risk screen** (§H); candidate-level scrutiny (non-PP + first-attempt), fenced-first |
| **Phase 2 — SEPARATELY GATED (not opened now)** | **identifiable adults** with controlled representation | yes, per-asset only | **evidence-based rights rule (§B)** + **disability-led representation review (§C)** + PK visual; commissioned OR stock **only with asset-specific contextual release evidence**; **no claim the person is an actual participant** unless expressly authorised |
| **Phase 3 — SEPARATELY GOVERNED + HELD** | identifiable **minors** · **real participant stories** · **clinical / personal-care / distress** contexts · **First Nations-specific** people, artwork, Country or cultural material · imagery revealing identifiable health/support information | **NO — purpose-bound only (§F)** | held; each attaches to an **individually approved story/campaign**, never the general reusable pool, even after approval |

**Rule A1 — Phase 0/1 enter the rotation pool; Phase 3 never does (§F).** **Rule A2 — a candidate ambiguous between phases classifies to the stricter phase.** **Rule A3 — the cultural screen (§D) and the permanent exclusions (§E) run over EVERY candidate in EVERY phase**, including person-free Phase 1 (an accessible-transport photo could still contain provider signage, a number plate, or a culturally sensitive motif).

### §B — Evidence-based rights rule for identifiable people (Ruling 1; Phase 2)

- **B1 — Prohibited by default.** Identifiable-people imagery does not proceed unless the **exact asset** carries documented rights.
- **B2 — What the evidence must cover (asset-specific):** image & likeness use · commercial **and social-media** publication · **disability / accessibility / NDIS-adjacent context** specifically · an explicit position that the image makes **no false statement that the person is an NDIS participant** · permitted **duration and territories** · any **withdrawal, expiry, or reuse restrictions** · guardian authority where applicable (**note:** identifiable minors are excluded from Phase 1 **and** Phase 2 — they are Phase 3, §A).
- **B3 — A general platform copyright licence or payment is NOT sufficient by itself.** Paid stock **without** adequate release evidence still **fails**. Free stock **with** independently verifiable adequate evidence could *theoretically* pass (expected to be uncommon). The gate is the evidence, not the price.
- **B4 — Compliance fit.** Consistent with NDIS Yarns' strict posture (general-info-not-advice, no promo, no Care-for-Welfare, no implied endorsement/outcomes/service relationship).

### §C — People-representation review + disability-led reviewer (Ruling 3; Phase 2)

Identifiable-people candidates require, **before PK's final visual decision**, a recorded review by a **PK-designated reviewer with disability-representation or lived-experience grounding** — ideally a paid external / disability-led adviser (cf. PWDA's "by and for" model). The reviewer returns a recorded verdict:

- **PASS** · **PASS WITH RESTRICTIONS** · **REJECT**

The reviewer assesses dignity and authenticity: everyday, strengths-based, agency-forward representation; breadth of disability (including non-visible); no stereotype/tokenism/prop framing. **PK retains the final brand decision; the image agents cannot substitute for this review, and this review sits above the agents and below PK.**

### §D — Cultural path — standing adviser + community-specific consultation (Ruling 4)

- **D1 — No universal authority.** There is **no single person who can approve every First Nations image** (Style Manual: authority is community / Traditional-Owner / elder / subject specific). The prior brief's "one PK-designated cultural authority" is **replaced**.
- **D2 — Two-part mechanism:** (i) a **standing First Nations adviser** for initial triage and protocol design; **plus** (ii) **community-specific consultation** with the relevant person, community, Traditional Owners, or cultural rights-holder whenever an image, artwork, location, cultural expression, or story is community-specific. Where a person is *also* identifiable, documented personal permission (§B) **and** cultural review both apply.
- **D3 — ICIP / rights trap.** "Aboriginal art" and cultural motifs on stock sites are almost always an authenticity + Indigenous Cultural and Intellectual Property (ICIP) trap; cultural review is **in addition to**, never a substitute for, the artist's own permission and normal rights clearance.
- **D4 — Current state = HARD BLOCK.** Until the D2 mechanism exists (standing adviser named + consultation route defined), **culturally specific imagery remains hard-blocked** — honestly, not aspirationally. This brief does not lift the block; it defines what lifting it requires. Evidence (adviser identity, consultation record, permissions) retained per-asset in `asset_meta`.

### §E — Permanent exclusions for generic background rotation (Ruling 5)

These are **permanently excluded** from any generic/rotating background — no phase, no approval, admits them into the pool:

- identifiable **minors**;
- **pity, tragedy, helplessness** framing, or **"inspirational hero"** framing solely because someone has a disability;
- **clinical gaze** close-ups of bodies/impairments · **personal care · distress · restraint · medical treatment**;
- a **support worker dominating** while the disabled person is passive;
- **implied diagnosis or participant status** without explicit contextual permission;
- **official NDIS logos** in photos · provider signage suggesting NDIS endorsement · uniforms/branded environments implying affiliation · anything making NDIS Yarns look like an **NDIA publication** (NDIA logo/affiliation restrictions);
- any image implying the person **endorses** NDIS Yarns or a provider;
- **identifiable records, medication, service documents, addresses, number plates,** or other personal information;
- **tokenistic or purely decorative** disability representation;
- images **unrelated to the article** used merely to signal "disability" (Style Manual: visual content must meet a genuine user need, not decorate).

### §F — Purpose-binding for high-sensitivity imagery (Ruling 6)

**Real participant stories and culturally specific imagery must remain purpose-bound** to an individually approved story or campaign. They **must not automatically enter a general reusable background pool**, even after approval. Phase 3 assets are attached to the specific approved purpose that justified them; reuse in another context requires a fresh gate. (This is why Phase 3 answers "no" to the rotation-pool column in §A.)

### §G — Sources & exclusions (reframed off the evidence rule)

- **Phase 0/1 sources — unchanged allow-list:** Unsplash standard / Pexels / Wikimedia CC0, per the six ratified licence rows. Phase 1 is **person-free / non-identifying** and passes the identity-risk screen (§H).
- **Phase 2 sources — evidence-gated, per-asset PK-elected:** commissioned-with-release (preferred, cleanest) **or** stock (free or paid) **only where asset-specific contextual release evidence exists (§B)**. Price is not the gate; evidence is.
- **Standing exclusions (unchanged + extended):** AI-generated people (excluded, with special force for synthetic disabled people) · CC BY / CC BY-SA · auction/crowd imagery · readable third-party signage/branding · everything in §E · any First-Nations content absent §D.

### §I — Charter deltas for the image agents

- **I1 — image-harvester:** its no-identifiable-people / no-faces exclusion is **reaffirmed and made NDIS-explicit** — it sources **Phase 1 person-free / non-identifying** NDIS backgrounds at candidate-level scrutiny (fenced-first), and **does not and cannot** source Phase 2/3 identifiable-people, participant, clinical, or cultural imagery (no release conveyance on the allow-list; out of the proven fence).
- **I2 — image-reviewer:** extend its output with an **identity-leak + representation/dignity + §E-exclusion + cultural-flag checklist** (suggestive only). New mandatory rule: **any First-Nations / culturally sensitive element → ESCALATE (§D); never return PASS.** It never approves; PK's visual verdict remains the only deciding act, and for Phase 2 the disability-led reviewer (§C) sits between the agent and PK.
- **I3 — standing scope condition:** identifiable-people / participant / cultural imagery runs at **candidate-level scrutiny + mandatory human review** (§C disability-led reviewer; §D cultural consultation) on every attempt — never automatable, never made routine by repetition. The agents never own GATES 1b/3/§D/5/6.

### §J — Prerequisite-status table (PK-directed; the anti-drift control)

**Governing rule (non-negotiable): an unfilled specialist role is NEVER permission to proceed.** The *absence* of an appointed §C reviewer or §D adviser means the dependent phase stays **closed / hard-blocked** — it can never be read as "the role is vacant, therefore the step is skipped." No lane may self-authorise past a held prerequisite; only a separate, explicit PK gate that records the prerequisite as **met** unlocks the dependent phase. A future lane that finds a prerequisite below marked HELD/BLOCKED must STOP and surface to PK.
