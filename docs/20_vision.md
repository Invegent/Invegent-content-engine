# Invegent — Vision Document
## Last updated: April 2026
## Type: Internal working document

---

## What Invegent Is

Invegent is an AI-operated content intelligence engine that transforms raw information streams into branded, platform-ready content for regulated and domain-specific industries — and publishes it automatically.

It is not a content scheduling tool. It is not a social media manager. It is not a template filler.

The fundamental difference: ICE starts upstream. Before a post is written, ICE has already ingested the signal that justifies it — a policy update, a market movement, a research finding, a trending topic. Every piece of content is traceable back to something real. That is the core.

---

## The Three Businesses ICE Serves

ICE was built to solve a content problem across three of PK's own businesses. External clients are a fourth application — a bonus, not the driver.

**Priority 1 — Care for Welfare**
Mobile allied health NDIS practice. OT-led. ICE runs NDIS Yarns on autopilot — building digital authority, generating referrals, growing the practice without consuming founder time. The measure of success: inbound referrals from support coordinators who follow the page.

**Priority 2 — Personal brand and creator channel**
YouTube presence, personal content series, property investment content through Property Pulse. ICE proves itself on PK's own world before asking anyone else to trust it. Property Pulse is also the validator for a potential property business pivot — if the page reaches meaningful traction within 12 months, the pivot has market proof.

**Priority 3 — Invegent (ICE as a product)**
The managed content service for external clients. NDIS providers first. Property professionals second. The entertainment/creator tier third. External clients benefit from everything proved on PK's own businesses first.

**This priority order never reverses.** A decision that adds complexity to ICE without serving the priority 1 and 2 use cases is the wrong decision at the current stage.

## What the World Looks Like When Invegent Succeeds

**At 12 months:** A solo CPA with no development background has built and operates an AI content system serving 8–10 paying clients, generating $8,000–15,000/month, with less than 10 hours/week of operational time. The system is proven on his own businesses first. The first external clients are NDIS providers who would not trust a generic content agency but will trust someone who operates inside their sector.

**At 3 years:** Invegent has transitioned from a managed service to a self-serve platform for regulated industries. Any GP, OT, physiotherapist, accountant, financial planner, or support coordinator in Australia can sign up, connect their social accounts, select their profession, and ICE generates compliant content from day one — without PK being in the loop. The compliance rule generator (AI reads the code of conduct, generates the rules) makes each new profession a one-day extension, not months of manual research.

**At 5 years:** The creator/entertainment tier is live. Aspirant YouTube channel operators — people who want to build a following but don't know where to start — use ICE as their full production pipeline: subscribe to channels that inspire them, let ICE analyse what works, generate scripts in their voice, render in their avatar, publish to YouTube automatically. The regulated professional market and the creator market run on the same infrastructure with different pricing.

## The Moat

ICE's competitive advantage is not the technology. The technology is available to anyone.

The moat is **vertical depth plus founder domain immersion**.

An NDIS provider walking into a conversation with PK is talking to someone who:
- Manages NDIS plans professionally
- Runs a mobile allied health practice
- Understands what providers can and cannot say publicly
- Has built compliance rules into the AI from the inside
- Has been generating content for NDIS Yarns for six months

No generic content agency can replicate this. No software-only tool can replicate this. The combination of domain knowledge, operational credibility, and working product is the moat.

The same principle applies to the creator tier — the inspiration pipeline, the avatar recreation, the video analyser — these are architectural decisions informed by knowing what creators actually need, not what product managers imagine they need.

---

## What Invegent Will Never Become

- A horizontal tool for any business in any industry. Vertical depth is the moat. Diluting it with breadth defeats the purpose.
- A self-serve platform before the managed service is proven. Build in the right order.
- A company that builds features before it has proof the current features work. The feedback loop must close before the next layer is added.
- A tool that publishes content without human approval on the record. ICE generates. Humans approve. The auto-approver is a productivity tool, not a removal of human accountability.
- A product that over-promises on compliance. ICE is compliance-aware. It is not a guarantee. The ToS must say this clearly.

## North Star Metric

**Operational hours per client per week.**

Today: ~3–4 hours/week per client (manual).
Target: under 30 minutes/week per client (automated).
At 30 minutes/week and 10 clients: 5 hours/week total operational time.

Every build decision should move this metric. If it doesn't, it is not a priority.

Revenue and client count are lag indicators. Operational efficiency is the lead indicator. A product that generates $15,000/month but requires 60 hours/week is not a success — it is a job with extra steps.

## How the Vision Gets Revised

This document is reviewed quarterly. It changes when:
- A new business direction is confirmed (e.g. Property Pulse validates the property pivot)
- The SaaS transition decision is made
- The creator tier moves from vision to active build
- External market conditions change the competitive landscape significantly

It does not change because of a single conversation or a single idea. It changes when there is evidence.
