## The Problem ICE Solves

Every NDIS provider, allied health practice, and property professional
knows they should be active on social media. Most are not — not because
they do not care, but because the actual problem is not scheduling posts.
The problem is knowing what to post, why it matters to your audience,
and how to say it in a way that builds trust without accidentally
making claims you cannot back up.

Content tools like Buffer, Hootsuite, and Later solve the distribution
problem. They do not solve the content creation problem. You still need
to know what to write, find the signal, and produce the post.

For a solo OT running a mobile practice, or a support coordinator with
a full client load, that work never rises to the top of the to-do list.
The result: sporadic posting, generic content, or nothing at all.

ICE solves the upstream problem — intelligence first, posts second.

## What ICE Does

ICE is a content engine. It runs continuously in the background,
doing four things without human input:

**1. Monitors the information landscape in your sector.**
ICE ingests RSS feeds, government sources, industry bodies, and
peak organisations relevant to your vertical. For NDIS clients:
DSS updates, NDIS Commission notices, disability sector research,
advocacy organisation publications. For property clients: RBA decisions,
CoreLogic data, REIA reports, PropTrack commentary. New signals
arrive daily. ICE reads all of them.

**2. Scores and selects what actually matters.**
Not everything is worth a post. ICE scores each signal against your
client's content scope — which topics matter to their specific audience.
Only high-relevance signals progress. Low-relevance items are discarded.
The scoring system improves over time as performance data feeds back in.

**3. Writes the post in your client's voice.**
Each client has a content intelligence profile: who they are, how they
speak, what they never do, what compliance context applies, which
platforms they publish on and with what constraints. ICE uses this
profile to write posts that read like the client wrote them — not like
an AI wrote them, not like a generic marketing agency wrote them.

**4. Publishes automatically.**
Approved posts go out on schedule. No login required. No manual copying
and pasting. The client's page gets consistent, relevant, on-brand
content without the client spending time on it.

Human input is required for three things only: approving flagged drafts
that fall below the auto-approval threshold (takes under 30 minutes per
week per client), updating the client's content profile when their
messaging evolves, and reviewing the monthly performance summary.

## What Makes ICE Different

**Signal-centric, not post-centric.**
ICE starts with real-world events and works forward to content.
Every post is traceable to a source — a policy change, a data release,
a research finding. This means ICE produces content that is genuinely
informative, not manufactured noise. Clients can stand behind what ICE
writes because it is grounded in real information.

**Vertical depth, not horizontal breadth.**
ICE is not a tool for any business. It is built for NDIS providers and
property professionals, with taxonomy, compliance context, and brand
profile depth that a generic content tool cannot replicate. The NDIS
taxonomy alone covers 2,582 specific topics across every domain of
disability services. A generic AI writing tool does not know what a
Registered Plan Manager can and cannot say publicly. ICE does.

**Built by an insider, not an agency.**
The person operating ICE is a CPA with 20 years of finance and analytics
experience, an NDIS Plan Manager, and the business administrator for a
mobile allied health practice. When ICE writes NDIS content, it is
calibrated by someone who operates inside the sector — not by a
marketing agency that read the NDIS website once.

**Managed service, not DIY software.**
ICE is not software you log into. You do not need to configure it,
learn a new tool, or manage a dashboard. You receive consistent,
on-brand, compliant content on your Facebook page. That is the
entire deliverable.

## What ICE Produces

### Signal Reactive Posts (daily)
Triggered by real-world events in the client's sector. When the NDIS
Commission publishes a new practice standard, when the RBA makes a rate
decision, when a landmark court ruling affects disability services —
ICE ingests the signal, scores its relevance, and produces a post
that gives the client's audience something useful within hours.

### Synthesised Commentary (daily)
When multiple signals on the same theme arrive together, ICE identifies
the connecting thread and writes a post that provides perspective,
not just reporting. This is where the practitioner voice matters most —
turning a cluster of sector signals into something the client's
audience can act on.

### Evergreen Education (planned — Phase 3)
Foundational educational content that rotates back into the schedule.
"What is an NDIS plan?" for new followers. "How does negative gearing
work?" for property audiences. New followers always encounter the
foundations. Existing followers see it infrequently enough that it
remains useful.

### Campaign Series (planned — Phase 3)
A structured 10-post series on a specific topic. "Everything you need
to know about the NDIS annual review process." Built from a brief,
reviewed before production, published on a defined schedule.

## What ICE Does Not Do

- Respond to comments, messages, or mentions
- Produce video content
- Create graphics or branded images
- Run full advertising campaigns (boosts only — single post promotion)
- Replace the client's own voice for personal or emotional posts
- Guarantee follower growth (ICE produces the content; distribution
  depends on platform dynamics, boost budget, and community strategy)
- Provide advice — ICE produces general information content, not
  advice specific to any individual's circumstances

## The Quality Standard

Every post ICE produces is held to the following standard before publishing:

- Starts with an insight or observation, not the name of the source
- Written in plain English — no jargon unless the audience expects it
- No markdown formatting (bold text, bullet lists, headers) in social posts
- No AI tell-phrases ("it's important to note", "in today's landscape")
- Includes a disclaimer where the topic requires one, woven naturally
  into the text rather than appended as boilerplate
- Ends with a specific, relevant question to the audience
- Does not make claims the client cannot stand behind
- Does not mention competitors, make personal recommendations,
  or give advice disguised as information

Drafts that do not meet this standard are flagged for human review
rather than auto-approved.
