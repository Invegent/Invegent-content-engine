### Hypothesis (one-paragraph summary; full findings in investigation file)

The 5 stuck rows have `attempt_count IS NULL` because they have **never passed the `eligible` CTE** in `m.publisher_lock_queue_v2` since being enqueued. Most likely cause: `cpp.max_per_day=2` filter excludes them on every publisher tick because the daily cap was already hit. Day-by-day for the past 7 days, PP-LinkedIn has published 2-3 posts at the very start of the UTC day (00:00:05-15 UTC), exhausting the cap immediately and leaving the rest of the UTC day with `published_today >= max_per_day`.

### Striking secondary anomaly

The 3 queue_ids referenced by `m.post_publish` for "today's" PP-LinkedIn publishes (`c2fdafe6...`, `3785396b...`, `2a117aa2...`) **do not exist in `m.post_publish_queue` at all** — SELECT on those ids returns 0 rows in any status. Their post_publish records show `platform_post_id = zapier-li-{ms_epoch}` matching this EF's format, but the originating queue rows are gone. Either (a) they were hard-deleted post-publish, (b) they came from a non-publisher_lock_queue_v2 code path, or (c) `post_publish.queue_id` is a stale value. Whichever path, those 3 publishes still feed `stats.published_today` in the eligibility CTE and continue to exclude the 5 stuck rows.

### Proposed remediation direction (NOT applied — separate brief next session, post PK review)

1. **Lead investigation:** identify the source of the 3 daily 00:00 UTC PP-LinkedIn publishes whose queue_ids no longer exist. They are the underlying anomaly; understanding them is the prerequisite for remediation.
2. **Surgical:** temporarily raise `cpp.max_per_day` for PP-LinkedIn to 6-8 for one or two UTC days to drain the 5 stuck rows, then restore to 2.
3. **Reset scheduled_for** alone is not effective — see investigation file for why.
