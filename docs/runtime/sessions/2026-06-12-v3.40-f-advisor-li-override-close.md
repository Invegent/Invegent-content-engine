## 1. Lane summary

LinkedIn's ≈71.9% Advisor override rate is **not** caused by Facebook-format inheritance, is **not** a policy violation, and is **not** publish-blocking. It is **expected Advisor correction behaviour under current config**, caused by the stack of:

1. `preferred_format_linkedin` is NULL for every client;
2. `fill_pending_slots` falls back to `image_quote` (`COALESCE(format_preference[1],'image_quote')`) → 100% of LinkedIn slots stamped `image_quote`;
3. the LinkedIn Advisor prompt receives **no** platform preference/bias instruction (Facebook does — `preferred_format_facebook='image_quote'` injects a bias instruction, hence FB's lower 29.3%);
4. the Advisor correctly chooses content-fit formats, mostly `text`, for reactive/conversational LinkedIn content (its documented default-to-text rule);
5. LinkedIn supports `text` (`t."5.3_content_format".platform_support.linkedin=true`), so these corrections are valid and publishable. Instagram excludes `text`, which is why IG sits at 11.3% on the same `image_quote` slot default.

**All 46/46 LinkedIn override rows carried LinkedIn `m.post_publish` rows — published, not blocking production.**
