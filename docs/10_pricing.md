# ICE — Pricing Model

## Pricing Philosophy

ICE is priced as a managed content service, not as software.
The client is paying for outcomes (consistent, intelligent, compliant
content on their page) not for access to a tool.

The price must reflect:
- The value of the outcome (referrals, authority, time saved)
- The vertical depth and compliance calibration that differentiates ICE
- The ongoing operational cost of maintaining that quality per client
- A margin that supports the business at target scale

Do not discount on the first conversation. The price is set because
it is correct, not because there is room to move. A client who
negotiates hard before they have started is a signal worth noting.

## Standard Tiers

### Starter — $500/month

**What's included:**
- 3 posts per week (approximately 13 per month)
- Facebook only
- Signal reactive content (standard ICE pipeline)
- Auto-approval enabled — no manual review required from client
- Monthly performance email (total posts, top engagement)
- Brand profile build included (one-time at onboarding)
- Email support (response within 2 business days)

**Who it's for:**
Practitioners or small practices who want to start with something
manageable. They need consistency more than volume. Good entry point
for someone who is currently posting nothing or near-nothing.

**Operational cost:** approximately 30 minutes/week.

---

### Standard — $800/month

**What's included:**
- 5 posts per week (approximately 22 per month)
- Facebook + LinkedIn (once LinkedIn is live — Phase 2.3)
- Signal reactive + synthesised commentary
- Auto-approval with weekly review check-in from Invegent
- Monthly performance summary with brief commentary
- Brand profile build included
- Priority email support (response within 1 business day)
- Cross-promotion network inclusion (NDIS clients)

**Who it's for:**
The target client. Established practice, knows what they need,
wants consistent volume without platform limitations. Standard is
the right tier for most NDIS providers.

**Operational cost:** approximately 45 minutes/week.

---

### Premium — $1,500/month

**What's included:**
- Daily posts (approximately 30 per month)
- Facebook + LinkedIn + email newsletter (when live)
- Signal reactive + synthesised commentary + campaign series
- Monthly strategy call (30 minutes)
- Monthly performance report with analysis and recommendations
- Campaign series: one per quarter (10-post series on a defined topic)
- Brand profile build + quarterly refresh
- Priority support (response within 4 hours business hours)
- Cross-promotion network inclusion
- Facebook post boosting (client provides ad budget; ICE manages targeting)

**Who it's for:**
Practices actively building their profile as a sector authority.
They understand the long game — 12 months of daily content before
the referral pipeline shifts. Likely have some existing audience
and want to accelerate growth.

**Operational cost:** approximately 90 minutes/week including
strategy call prep.

## One-Time Fees

### Onboarding Fee — $300 (waived for first 3 clients)
Covers the brand profile build, content scope configuration,
platform setup, and 2-week calibration window.

Rationale: onboarding takes 4–6 hours of founder time. At target
automation levels, this drops to 2–3 hours. Until that target is
reached, the one-time fee partially offsets the setup cost.

For the first three clients, waive the fee entirely. Early clients
are paying a premium in trust — reward that with lower friction.

### Campaign Brief (ad hoc) — $200 per campaign
A 10-post campaign series outside the standard pipeline.
Client provides a brief (topic, audience, goal).
ICE produces the full series for review before publishing.
Available to Standard and Premium tiers only.

## Pricing Edge Cases

**Client wants more posts than their tier allows:**
Upgrade to the next tier. Do not add posts à la carte.
Per-post pricing creates the wrong incentive — more posts
is not always better, and ICE should not be rewarded for volume
at the expense of quality.

**Client wants to pause for 1 month:**
Allow one pause per year of up to 4 weeks at no charge.
Publishing stops; brand profile and configuration are preserved.
Second pause in the same year: 50% of monthly fee applies
to maintain the account.

**Client wants to pause indefinitely:**
Close the account. Offer to restart as a new client.
Do not hold dormant accounts open — they consume maintenance overhead.

**Client is a non-profit or very small practice (1 person):**
No standard discount. ICE's time cost is the same regardless
of client size. If they genuinely cannot afford $500/month,
they are not the right client yet. A practice billing $10k/month
can afford $500 for content.

**Client has multiple pages (e.g. personal brand + practice):**
Each page is a separate client profile and a separate account.
Discount: $100/month off the second account if both are on Standard
or above. Not available on Starter.

**Client wants a discount for paying annually:**
Offer 2 months free (equivalent to ~17% discount) for 12-month
upfront payment. Only offer this if the client raises it.
Do not proactively offer annual pricing.

## Unit Economics

At current automation levels (Phase 2):

| Tier | Revenue | Direct costs | Gross margin | Founder time |
|------|---------|-------------|-------------|-------------|
| Starter | $500 | ~$20 | ~$480 | ~2 hrs/month |
| Standard | $800 | ~$25 | ~$775 | ~3 hrs/month |
| Premium | $1,500 | ~$40 | ~$1,460 | ~6 hrs/month |

Direct costs: Claude API (~$10–20/month per client at current volume),
Vercel/Supabase pro-rated share (~$5), Facebook API (no direct cost).

At target automation (Phase 3+, with client portal self-service):
Founder time drops to 30–60 minutes per month per client.
At that point, 10 Standard clients = $8,000/month revenue at roughly
5–10 hours total monthly operational time.

## What Justifies the Price

When a client asks why ICE costs $800/month, the answer is not
"because it takes time to run." The answer is:

1. **Compliance depth.** A generic marketing agency will write
   NDIS content that makes you uncomfortable. ICE was built by
   someone who has operated inside the NDIS ecosystem and knows
   what providers can and cannot say publicly. That compliance
   calibration has real risk management value.

2. **Intelligence, not scheduling.** ICE monitors your sector
   every day and selects what matters. You are not paying for
   someone to schedule your posts — you are paying for the
   content intelligence layer that makes the posts worth reading.

3. **Time value.** At $800/month, that is $27/day. One OT session
   in a mobile practice bills at $150–$200/hour. If consistent
   social media presence generates one additional referral per month,
   ICE has already paid for itself. The question is not "is $800/month
   a lot" — the question is "what is one new client worth to your practice."

4. **Proof.** NDIS Yarns has been running on ICE since Phase 1.
   The output quality, volume, and consistency are demonstrable
   in real time. This is not a promise — it is a live example.
