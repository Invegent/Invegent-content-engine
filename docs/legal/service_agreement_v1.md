## 1. Definitions

**"ICE"** means the Invegent Content Engine, the AI-assisted content generation and publishing platform operated by Invegent.

**"Service"** means the content generation, scheduling, and publishing services described in the selected Service Package, as set out in Schedule 1.

**"Content"** means social media posts, captions, images, videos, newsletters, and any other material generated by ICE for publication on behalf of the Client.

**"Platform"** means any third-party social media or digital distribution service to which Content is published, including but not limited to Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, YouTube, and email platforms.

## 2. Nature of Service — Intermediary Role

2.1 Invegent operates as a **content intermediary only**. Invegent generates and publishes content on behalf of the Client but does not act as the Client's legal, compliance, or regulatory adviser.

2.2 The Client acknowledges that:

a) All Content published through the Service is published **on behalf of and under the authority of the Client**. The Client is the publisher of record for all purposes.

b) The Client retains **final responsibility** for all Content published under their name, including compliance with all applicable laws, regulations, industry codes, and Platform terms of service.

c) Invegent is not a registered NDIS provider, health practitioner, financial adviser, or legal adviser, and nothing in the Service or Content constitutes professional advice of any kind.

2.3 The Client is responsible for reviewing Content prior to publication where the Client has selected manual approval mode, and for monitoring published Content on an ongoing basis.

## 3. AI-Generated Content Disclosure

3.1 The Client acknowledges and agrees that Content generated through ICE is produced with the assistance of artificial intelligence tools, including large language models.

3.2 AI-generated content is reviewed for quality and relevance but is not individually authored by a human for each post. The Client accepts this as part of the Service model.

3.3 Where the Client's industry, platform, or audience requires disclosure of AI-generated content, **the Client is solely responsible for making any required disclosure**. Invegent does not make such disclosures on the Client's behalf.

3.4 The Client warrants that their use of AI-generated content complies with all applicable obligations under their professional registration, industry code of conduct, and any regulatory framework applicable to their business.

## 5. Platform Access

5.1 To enable publishing to the Client's social media pages, the Client must:

a) Grant Invegent's Meta application access to the relevant Facebook Page(s) and Instagram account(s) as instructed during onboarding.

b) Maintain the platform access grants in active status for the duration of the Agreement.

c) Notify Invegent promptly if platform access is revoked or changed.

5.2 **Invegent will never request, store, or use the Client's personal login credentials or passwords** for any Platform. All platform access is granted through official OAuth or developer API mechanisms.

5.3 If platform access is revoked by the Client or the Platform, publishing will cease. Invegent is not liable for any loss resulting from publishing interruption caused by access revocation, Platform policy changes, or Platform outages.

5.4 The Client is responsible for maintaining their Platform accounts in good standing, including compliance with Platform terms of service. Suspension of a Platform account for reasons attributable to the Client's conduct does not constitute a failure of service by Invegent.

## 6. Service Package and Scope

6.1 The Service Package selected by the Client at onboarding is set out in Schedule 1. The Service Package defines the platforms, content volume, and features included in the monthly fee.

6.2 **Package versioning:** Service Packages are versioned. Where Invegent improves a Service Package without increasing the monthly fee, existing clients will be notified of the upgrade. Clients may acknowledge the upgrade through the Client Portal. The signed service agreement will be updated to reflect the new package version, and the previous version will be retained as an audit record.

6.3 **Price changes:** If the monthly fee for a Service Package increases, existing clients will receive 60 days written notice before the new price takes effect. Clients may cancel without penalty during this notice period.

6.4 Content volume commitments (posts per week) are targets, not guarantees. Delivery may vary based on signal availability, platform conditions, and client-requested blackout periods.

## 8. Term and Cancellation

8.1 This Agreement commences on the date of signing and continues on a monthly basis until terminated.

8.2 Either party may cancel this Agreement by providing **30 days written notice** to the other party via email.

8.3 Upon receipt of a cancellation notice:

a) Invegent will continue to deliver the Service for the 30-day notice period.

b) Content scheduled or generated during the notice period will be published as normal unless the Client requests otherwise.

c) After the notice period expires, Invegent will cease publishing and revoke application access.

8.4 Invegent may terminate immediately and without notice if:

a) The Client uses the Service to publish unlawful, defamatory, misleading, or harmful content.

b) The Client is in material breach of this Agreement and has failed to remedy the breach within 7 days of written notice.

c) The Client becomes insolvent or enters administration.

8.5 No refunds are provided for partial months, except at Invegent's discretion.
