### 1. What Invegent will do

Invegent will operate a managed content service for the Client's nominated social media presence ("Service"). Specifically, Invegent will:

- **Publish content** to the Client's nominated Facebook Page (and optionally other platforms, see Schedule A) on a regular cadence, drawing from public industry news sources Invegent monitors.
- **Generate post drafts** using AI (Claude by Anthropic, OpenAI as fallback), passed through Invegent's compliance rules engine before being made available for review or auto-publishing.
- **Apply NDIS compliance rules** (or the relevant industry compliance rules listed in Schedule A) to all content before it is published.
- **Provide monthly reporting** covering what was published, posting cadence, and basic engagement metrics from the platform's own analytics.
- **Pause or stop publishing** at the Client's request within one (1) business day.

---

### 2. Pilot pricing and KPI guarantee

**Pilot fee — TO BE DECIDED CLOSER TO FIRST SALES CONVERSATION.**

Pricing is deliberately not locked in this template. PK will reassess based on product strength and weakness at the time of the first sales conversation. Current candidate options held here for future consideration:

- **Option A — Half-price pilot:** AUD $400 per month (50% of standard managed service rate of $800/month). Payable monthly in advance. Signals confidence; filters for clients who already see value.
- **Option B — Free trial pilot:** No fee for the first 3 months. From month 4 onwards, standard fee of AUD $800/month applies if Client chooses to continue. Lower friction; converts better; risk of cheapening perception.
- **Option C — Other.** A different structure (lower flat fee, success-based, performance-tiered, etc.) may be appropriate depending on the specific client and product maturity at the time.

**KPI guarantee — 50% off next month on miss:**

If, in any given calendar month after the first 14 days of service, Invegent fails to meet the following minimum operational KPI, the Client receives a 50% credit applied to the following month's invoice:

- **KPI: Posting cadence.** Average of at least four (4) posts per week, measured across any rolling 28-day window during the month, on the Client's primary nominated platform (Facebook Page).

**Reporting metrics (no KPI guarantee attached, for transparency only):**

- Average engagement rate per post (likes, comments, shares as percentage of reach)
- Net change in followers/Page Likes over the month
- Top-performing post for the month (by engagement)

These metrics are reported monthly but no specific outcome is warranted. Engagement and follower trajectories are influenced by factors outside Invegent's control (platform algorithms, audience size, seasonality, content topic).

### 3. What the Client agrees to

The Client agrees to:

- **Provide platform access.** Grant Invegent administrator access to the nominated Facebook Page (and other platforms as agreed) using the platform's standard sharing mechanism. Client may revoke access at any time.
- **Retain final approval rights.** The Client may review any post draft before it publishes by enabling "manual approval mode". The Client may reject or edit any draft, in which case Invegent will not publish it.
- **Respond to drafts within 48 hours** during business days, where manual approval mode is enabled. Drafts not actioned within 48 hours may be auto-rejected by the system.
- **Treat Invegent as a content service provider, not the publisher of record.** The Client is the author and publisher of the content from a regulatory and reputational standpoint.

---

### 4. AI disclosure

The Client acknowledges and agrees that:

- All content produced by the Service is **AI-assisted**. Posts are generated by large language models (currently Claude by Anthropic, with OpenAI as fallback) under Invegent's compliance-aware editorial rules.
- Posts are not human-written from scratch.
- The Client is responsible for **disclosing AI assistance** where required by the platform's terms of service, the Client's professional code of conduct, the Client's regulatory framework (including any NDIS Practice Standards applicable to the Client's services), or any other applicable disclosure obligation.
- Invegent will, on request, provide standard AI-disclosure language the Client may adopt for their platform bios or About sections.

### 5. Experimental service waiver

The Client acknowledges that:

- **Invegent is an early-stage service.** The technology is functional but evolving. The Client is not contracting for an established, production-grade enterprise service.
- **Service availability is not warranted.** Pauses, outages, slow periods, and temporary halts for fixes are possible and expected.
- **Performance outcomes are not warranted.** Invegent does not warrant any specific engagement rate, follower growth, lead conversion, sales attribution, or other performance result. Section 2 KPIs are operational (Invegent's deliverable) not outcome-based.
- **AI-generated content may occasionally contain errors.** Despite compliance rules and editorial controls, AI-generated content may occasionally produce factual errors, awkward phrasing, or content the Client would not have chosen. The Client retains final responsibility for what is published on its platforms.
- **Invegent's total aggregate liability** under this agreement is limited to the fees paid by the Client in the three (3) months preceding any claim. Invegent is not liable for indirect, consequential, or special damages, including reputation damage, lost business opportunity, or regulatory action arising from the Client's use of the Service.

### 6. Data handling

- Invegent's data handling is governed by the Invegent Privacy Policy at **invegent.com/privacy** (accessed [DATE OF SIGNING]).
- Client data is stored in Invegent's Supabase database, encrypted, hosted in the Asia Pacific (Sydney) region.
- Client data is shared with the AI service providers (Anthropic, OpenAI) and infrastructure providers (Supabase, Vercel) only to the extent necessary to deliver the Service. No data is sold or shared with third parties for marketing purposes.
- The Client may request deletion of all Client data at any time by emailing pk@invegent.com. Deletion is completed within 30 days, except for aggregate statistics that do not identify the Client.

### 7. Termination

- Either party may terminate this agreement with seven (7) days written notice (email to the addresses in the header), at any time, for any reason.
- On termination, Invegent will:
  - Cease publishing within one (1) business day of notice
  - Remove its administrator access to the Client's platforms
  - Provide the Client with a copy of all post drafts produced during the engagement (in markdown format), if requested
  - Delete the Client's data within 30 days, per Section 6
- Outstanding fees are payable up to the date of termination.
- The KPI credit (Section 2) does not apply on the final invoice if termination is initiated by the Client.
