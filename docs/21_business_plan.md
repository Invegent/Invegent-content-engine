# Invegent — Business Plan
## Last updated: April 2026
## Type: Internal working document — honest, not investor-facing

---

## The Problem

Small businesses in regulated industries — NDIS providers, allied health practices, property professionals, accountants, financial planners — know they need consistent social media presence. They do not have time to produce it. They fear getting the compliance wrong in public. They cannot afford to hire a content agency that doesn't understand their sector.

The result: most have no social media presence, or a presence that's embarrassing, or they're paying a generic agency $500/month for content that could get them into trouble with their regulator.

The specific pain for NDIS providers is sharper than most: the sector is compliance-heavy (NDIS Practice Standards, Disability Discrimination Act, state legislation), the consequences of getting it wrong in public communications are real (complaints, registration conditions, professional embarrassment), and the providers who most need to build authority — small OT practices, new support coordination businesses — have the least time and the smallest budgets for marketing.

---

## The Solution

ICE (Invegent Content Engine) is a done-for-you managed content service that:

1. Monitors the sector for relevant signals (policy updates, research, trends, events)
2. Generates platform-specific content in the client's brand voice using AI
3. Routes drafts through compliance checks specific to the client's profession
4. Publishes approved content automatically on a schedule
5. Tracks performance and feeds results back into content improvement

The client does not write, schedule, or manage content. They approve flagged drafts (typically under 30 minutes/week) and ICE handles the rest.

The differentiator: ICE was built by an OT and NDIS Plan Manager. The compliance rules, the content taxonomy, the brand voice guidelines — all designed by someone operating inside the sector, not observing it from outside.

## Market Sizing

**Primary market — NDIS Providers (Australia)**
- Registered NDIS providers: approximately 18,000 active as of 2026
- Target segment: small to medium practices (2–20 staff), founder-led, in allied health or support services
- Addressable segment (those with digital marketing budget): estimated 3,000–5,000
- Realistic penetration in 3 years: 50–100 clients
- Revenue at 100 clients (Standard tier): $80,000/month

**Secondary market — Property Professionals (Australia)**
- Mortgage brokers, buyers agents, real estate agencies, property educators
- Total: approximately 15,000 licensed professionals
- Target: those building a personal brand or referral practice
- Addressable: 2,000–4,000

**Creator/Entertainment tier (Global) — a core ICE output tier, not aspirational**
- Aspirant YouTube channel operators, personal brand builders, faceless channel creators
- The creator economy is estimated at $191 billion in 2026
- ICE's position: managed service for channel setup and video content production
- Pricing: separate tier ($99–299/month), different cost structure (higher AI render cost via HeyGen)
- Build sequence: video pipeline layers (short-form → avatar → long-form episode)
- This tier runs on the same infrastructure as professional services with different pricing

**Future market — Regulated verticals beyond NDIS (Australia, then global)**
- Aged care, mental health, legal services, accounting, financial planning
- Each vertical requires: compliance rule research, taxonomy extension, proof client in the vertical
- The AI compliance rule generator (ANZSCO occupation tasks + profession code of conduct → rules) makes this scalable without manual research per profession
- Each new vertical is a 1–2 week extension, not a rebuild

## Competitive Landscape

**Direct competitors — generic content tools:**
Buffer, Hootsuite, Later, Sprout Social. These solve the distribution problem. They do not solve the content creation problem. They assume content exists and need scheduling. ICE assumes nothing and generates everything.

**Adjacent competitors — AI content generators:**
Jasper, Copy.ai, InVideo AI. These generate content but have no vertical depth, no compliance awareness, no managed service layer. A GP using InVideo AI gets generic health content. A GP using ICE gets NDIS-compliant allied health content reviewed against 22 profession-specific rules.

**The managed service gap:**
No managed content service exists that combines AI generation with genuine vertical domain knowledge and compliance awareness for regulated Australian industries. This is the gap ICE occupies.

## Business Model

**Tier 1 — Starter: $500/month**
- 3 posts per week
- Facebook only
- Text and image formats
- Monthly performance summary
- Best for: new practices, early-stage providers

**Tier 2 — Standard: $800/month**
- 5 posts per week
- Facebook + LinkedIn (when approved)
- All formats including video shorts
- Weekly draft review (under 30 min)
- Best for: established practices wanting consistent presence

**Tier 3 — Premium: $1,500/month**
- Daily posts
- All platforms
- Content series (multi-episode campaigns)
- Monthly analytics report
- Best for: practices actively investing in growth

**Video Add-on: +$300–500/month**
- YouTube channel content
- Avatar video generation (HeyGen)
- YouTube channel subscriptions as inspiration feeds
- Video analysis and recreation pipeline
- Requires HeyGen avatar setup (2–5 min footage)

**Source allocation (ICE's differentiator):**
Each tier includes a defined number of RSS/newsletter signal sources monitored. More sources = richer, more current content. Premium clients get more feeds, more newsletter subscriptions, higher signal density.

## Unit Economics

| Item | Monthly cost |
|---|---|
| Claude API (content generation, 5 posts/week x 4 weeks) | ~$15 |
| Creatomate (image/video renders) | ~$5 (shared across clients) |

| Vercel (shared infrastructure) | ~$1–2 per client share |
| Resend (email notifications) | ~$1 |
| YouTube Data API | Free quota |
| **Total direct cost** | **~$25–30/month** |
| **Gross margin** | **~96–97%** |

Founder time at target (30 min/week per client):
- 10 clients = 5 hours/week operational time
- At $800/month average: $8,000/month revenue
- Effective hourly rate: $1,600/hour of operational work

The unit economics only work at this margin if the automation targets are met. The auto-approver and feed intelligence are not features — they are what makes the business model viable at 10+ clients.

## Revenue Position — Prove First, Then Forecast

Revenue targets have deliberately not been set for 12+ months out. The reason is honest: forecasting subscription revenue before the product has been tested with paying external clients produces numbers that feel certain but are not. A forecast made before proof is marketing, not planning.

**The current position:**
- Zero external revenue. Two internal test clients (NDIS Yarns, Property Pulse) running on the pipeline.
- The pipeline works. The product is not yet proven with paying clients.
- The 12-month goal is product proof, not a revenue number.

**What "product proven" means:**
- 3+ paying external clients retained for 3+ consecutive months
- Operational time genuinely under 2 hours/week total across all clients
- At least one client who can provide a reference
- Performance data showing the content is producing page growth (even modest growth)

**When to set revenue targets:**
Once the product is proven by the above criteria, revenue targets will be set based on actual retention rates, actual client acquisition velocity, and actual operational capacity — not modelled assumptions.

**The near-term gate (next 3 months):**
- Meta App Review Standard Access confirmed
- NDIS Yarns proof document ready (whatever data exists at that point)
- 5 specific NDIS providers identified for first conversations
- First client conversation happens

Everything beyond that is determined by what the market tells us, not what a spreadsheet says.

## SaaS Transition Criteria

The transition from managed service to self-serve SaaS is the most important strategic decision ICE will make. It should not be made based on ambition — it should be made based on evidence.

**Trigger conditions (all must be true):**
1. 10+ clients served successfully for 3+ consecutive months
2. Operational time per client is genuinely under 30 minutes/week (not aspirational)
3. The compliance rule generator is built and has been validated on at least 2 professions beyond NDIS OT
4. Unit economics at managed service scale are confirmed (actual P&L, not modelled)
5. There is evidence of demand from potential clients who cannot afford the managed service price

**If these conditions are not met by 24 months:** continue managed service, add a part-time team member, do not force the SaaS transition before it's ready.

**If these conditions are met:** design the self-serve onboarding flow, build the client-facing AI compliance rule setup, define the support infrastructure, and pilot with 5 self-serve clients before broad launch.

## Funding Position

Invegent is entirely self-funded. No external investment is planned or required to reach the 10-client milestone. The infrastructure cost is low enough that revenue from 2–3 clients covers all running costs.

If external investment is ever considered, it would be to fund:
1. The SaaS transition (engineering resources for self-serve onboarding)
2. Sales and marketing at scale
3. Legal and compliance infrastructure for additional jurisdictions

At the current stage, investor involvement would add governance overhead without adding capability that the business actually needs. Revisit at the SaaS transition decision point.
